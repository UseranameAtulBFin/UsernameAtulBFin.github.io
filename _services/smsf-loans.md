# Property Investment Through Your SMSF

SMSF property loans — known as Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangements (LRBAs) — allow your super fund to borrow money to purchase a property. It's a specialist area that requires careful planning and the right lender.

## Key Features of SMSF Lending

- **Limited recourse** — the lender's recourse is limited to the asset itself, protecting other fund assets
- **Bare trust structure** — a separate holding trust is required to hold the property until the loan is paid off
- **Eligible properties** — the property must meet the sole purpose test and comply with super law
- **Competitive lenders** — not all lenders offer SMSF loans; Atul works with those that do

## What to Consider

SMSF borrowing involves additional complexity and cost compared to standard investment lending. Atul works alongside your financial adviser and accountant to ensure the strategy is right for your fund and retirement goals.

- Minimum fund balance requirements (typically $200,000+)
- Ongoing compliance and accounting obligations
- Property type restrictions
- Loan-to-value ratio (LVR) limits
